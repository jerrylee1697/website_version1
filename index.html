California Plug Load Research Center

Undergraduate Research Assistant

As an undergraduate research assistant in the CalPlug lab, I am working with a team of engineers to build solutions that can externally attach to, retrofit, and extend upon existing consumer electronics to perform energy saving actions while minimizing human intervention. Firmware and hardware we use include the ESP32, MQTT IoT protocol, various sensors using I2C communication protocol, FreeRTOS, and Eclipse.

UC Irvine Micromouse

Robotics Coordinator

Following my admission to UC Irvine, I founded UC Irvine's first micromouse division. I wanted to share the experience I gained from micromouse and create a community of aspiring engineers with common interest. I instruct other students in hardware using micro-controllers, various sensors, motors, encoders, and electrical circuit concepts. Software concepts include Proportional Integral Derivative (PID) Control, Pulse Width Modulation (PWM), and path-finding algorithms. My goal as the robotics coordinator is to have multiple teams representing UC Irvine at collegiate robotics competitions succeed.
